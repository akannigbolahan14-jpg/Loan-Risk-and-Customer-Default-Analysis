# Loan-Risk-and-Customer-Default-Analysis

## DATA ANALYSIS PROJECT BRIEF

### Introduction

CrediTrust is a lending institution facing increasing loan defaults, weak customer risk identification, and revenue losses from unpaid loans. To improve lending decisions and reduce financial exposure, a data-driven analysis was conducted on customer loan records.
The objective of this project was to analyse customer borrowing behaviour, identify factors associated with loan default, and provide actionable recommendations to improve loan approval processes and overall risk management.

### Project Objectives
The analysis focused on the following objectives:

* Clean and prepare the loan dataset

* Identify patterns behind customer loan defaults

* Analyze high-risk and low-risk customer behavior

* Evaluate financial risk indicators

* Segment customers into risk categories

* Provide business recommendations to reduce default rates
  
### Data Cleaning & Preparation
Several data preprocessing steps were performed to improve data quality and ensure reliable analysis.
Cleaning Activities Performed
•
Removed duplicate records
•
Handled missing values appropriately
•
Standardized categorical entries
•
Corrected inconsistent formatting
•
Converted data types where necessary
•
Treated extreme outliers in financial columns
•
Created additional analytical features such as installment burden ratio and age groups
Outcome

The cleaned dataset became more accurate, consistent, and suitable for financial analysis and business decision-making.

### Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Exploratory analysis was conducted to understand customer borrowing behavior and loan characteristics.
Key Findings
1. Average Loan Amount
The analysis revealed the average amount customers borrow, helping management understand overall lending exposure.
2. Employment Status and Loan Size
Certain employment groups requested significantly larger loans, indicating differences in borrowing behaviour and repayment capacity.
Credit Score Distribution
Credit scores showed varying levels of customer financial reliability. Lower credit scores were more common among high-risk borrowers.
Most Common Loan Purpose
Personal and business-related loans represented a major share of customer borrowing activity.
Age Group Borrowing Patterns
Middle-aged customers tended to request larger loan amounts compared to younger borrowers.
Income vs Loan Amount
A positive relationship existed between income and loan amount, although some low-income customers were taking relatively large loans, increasing financial risk.
5. Risk Analysis
A detailed financial risk analysis was performed to identify the strongest indicators of customer default.
Major Risk Indicators Identified
Low Credit Score
Customers with low credit scores demonstrated significantly higher default tendencies.
Previous Default History
Borrowers with previous default records showed a much higher probability of defaulting again.
High Instalment Burden
Customers spending a large proportion of their income on loan repayments experienced higher financial stress and repayment difficulty.
Unstable Employment
Unemployed customers and customers with unstable income sources showed elevated default risk.
Large Loans Relative to Income
Customers taking loans beyond their repayment capacity posed greater financial risk to the company.
6. Customer Segmentation
Customers were segmented into three major risk groups:
Risk Category
Characteristics
High Risk
Low credit score, previous default history, unstable employment, high repayment burden
Medium Risk
Moderate credit profile and manageable financial behaviour
Low Risk
Strong credit score, stable income, low repayment burden
Business Importance
This segmentation helps management:
•
prioritize low-risk customers
•
reduce exposure to risky borrowers
•
improve loan approval decisions
•
strengthen portfolio management
7. Business Recommendations
Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were proposed:
Tighten Approval Criteria
Customers with:
•
low credit scores
•
previous defaults
•
unstable employment
should undergo stricter approval checks.
Reduce Loan Exposure for Risky Customers
Loan amounts should be reduced for customers with:
•
low income
•
high instalment burden
•
unstable repayment capacity
Improve Customer Monitoring
High-risk borrowers should receive:
•
closer repayment monitoring
•
automated payment reminders
•
periodic financial reviews
Implement Risk-Based Lending
Different customer categories should receive different loan conditions based on risk level.
8. Business Impact
Implementing these recommendations can help CrediTrust:
•
reduce loan default rates
•
improve loan recovery performance
•
minimize financial losses
•
improve profitability
•
strengthen lending decisions
•
build a healthier loan portfolio
9. Conclusion
The analysis successfully identified the major drivers of loan default and highlighted important financial risk patterns among customers.
Key factors influencing default included:
•
low credit scores
•
previous default history
•
unstable employment
•
high repayment burden
Through effective data analysis and customer risk segmentation, CrediTrust can improve its loan approval strategy, reduce financial exposure, and make more informed lending decisions.
Overall, the project demonstrates how data analytics can support smarter financial risk management and improve organizational profitability.
